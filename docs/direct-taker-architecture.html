7. Key Concepts at a Glance

STEP 1 Order Collector

Subscribes to AddOrderV3/RemoveOrderV3/TakeOrderV3 events. Filters by token pair (USDC + configured wtTokens) and excluded_owner. Persists TrackedOrder aggregates keyed by order hash. Backfills from deployment_block on first run.

STEP 2 Order Classification

Decompiles Rainlang bytecode from AddOrderV3 to classify orders: fixed-price constants (literal IO ratio) or Pyth oracle pricing (reads price feed). Unsupported patterns are skipped. This determines HOW to evaluate profitability.

STEP 3 Profitability Strategy

Continuously re-evaluates ALL active orders each cycle. Compares order's IO ratio against Alpaca price, including all costs (gas, fees, mint/redeem, slippage). Uses hold pool inventory to reduce costs. Must exceed min_margin.

STEP 4 Token Acquisition

Scenario A: just needs USDC (simple). Scenario B: check hold pool first, otherwise JIT mint (buy AAPL on Alpaca -> mint tAAPL -> wrap to wtAAPL). The mint latency is the critical risk -- order may disappear during the wait.

CRITICAL IO Ratio Semantics

Raindex IO ratio is from the ORDER OWNER's perspective. When we take: their output = our input, their input = our output. Scenario A: our buy price = 1/io_ratio. Scenario B: our sell price = io_ratio. Inverting this wrong = guaranteed losses.

NEW Hold Pool

wtTokens sitting in the bot's wallet (not in Raindex vaults). Fed by: Scenario A takes (received wtTokens) and orphaned Scenario B mints (order disappeared during mint). Drained by: profitable Scenario B takes or time-based redemption.

SHARED Tokenization Flow

Reuses existing Tokenizer trait + AlpacaTokenizationService. Same as rebalancer EXCEPT: no vault deposit/withdrawal steps. Taker holds tokens in wallet, not Raindex vaults. takeOrders4 uses ERC20 transferFrom directly from wallet.

NEW Re-validation After Mint

After JIT minting in Scenario B, the bot MUST re-check: (1) order still exists? (2) still profitable at actual fill price? (3) simulation passes? If any check fails, tokens go to hold pool -- never waste gas on a doomed take.
